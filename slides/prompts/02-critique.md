Critique `talk_design/outline.md` as a hostile CAiSE PC member.

Be specific and uncomfortable. Diplomatic feedback is useless here.

## Five passes

1. **Overclaim audit.** For every results claim on a slide, point at the wording and
   say where it goes beyond what the data licenses. Watch for: "best," "always,"
   "universally," "paradigm," "no X" (when "no X to our knowledge" is the truth).

2. **"Did you control for X?"** List the top 5 experimental-design vulnerabilities a
   hostile reviewer would prosecute, ranked by severity. For PMF specifically, watch:
   baseline selection, horizon choice, aggregation granularity, univariate vs multivariate,
   statistical significance, TSFM checkpoint freshness.

3. **Weakest narrative link.** Identify the single load-bearing inference the talk
   depends on. If a skeptic refuses to buy that inference, the talk falls apart.
   Where is it, and what's the rebuttal sentence the speaker must memorize?

4. **Q&A worst questions.** The 5 questions the speaker would least want to be asked
   from this audience, ranked by danger. For each, give a one-sentence answer to
   rehearse cold.

5. **Hidden vocabulary.** Every term used on a slide that the audience may not actually
   know. For each, propose the smallest inline definition that fits the slide budget.

## For each issue, propose exactly one fix

- **(a) Slide-level fix** — change the text or visual on a specific slide
- **(b) Framing change** — change how the contribution is stated
- **(c) Concede in talk** — acknowledge head-on, with the exact sentence to say

- PMF is NOT assumed known; "foundation model" defaults to LLM

## End with a punch list

## What good looks like

Specific, actionable, citation-of-paper-page when applicable. Do not balance positives;
this prompt is for sharpening, not validation.
